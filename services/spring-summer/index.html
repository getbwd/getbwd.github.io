🌿 Spring & Summer in Michigan

The Season Moves Fast — Don't Fall Behind

Michigan's growing season is short and unpredictable. A wet spring can mean your lawn goes from fine to a foot tall in two weeks. Weeds get a foothold fast. Beds that looked okay in April are overrun by June.

The earlier you get on a schedule, the easier the whole season is — for you and for your lawn.

Early Spring

Yard cleanup, aeration, pre-emergent weed control, first mow

Late Spring

Regular mowing schedule, fertilization, bed care underway

Summer

Ongoing mowing & trimming, debris removal, weed control touch-ups

Late Summer / Fall

Overseeding, fall fertilization, final cleanups before winter
