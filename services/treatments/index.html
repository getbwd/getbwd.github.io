01

Aeration

Your lawn can't breathe if the soil is compacted. Aeration fixes that.

Over time — especially in yards with heavy foot traffic or clay-heavy soil (common in Genesee County) — the ground gets packed down tight. Water, air, and nutrients can't get to the roots. The grass thins out, gets patchy, and stops recovering from heat or drought the way it should.

Core aeration pulls small plugs of soil out of the ground and creates channels for everything to get through. It's one of the single best things you can do for a struggling lawn.

  • ✓ Core aeration across the full lawn
  • ✓ Cores left to break down naturally (they feed the lawn)
  • ✓ Best done in fall or early spring for cool-season grasses
  • ✓ Often paired with overseeding for maximum results

02

Overseeding

Thin spots, bare patches, or grass that just looks worn out — overseeding fills it back in.

We spread seed directly into your existing lawn without tearing anything up. The new grass fills in thin areas, thickens up the overall turf, and crowds out weeds over time. It's less invasive than sodding and less expensive — and when done right, the results last.

Timing matters a lot with seeding. In Michigan, late summer into early fall is the sweet spot for most cool-season grasses. We'll let you know if your window is right.

  • ✓ Seed selection matched to your lawn's sun exposure and soil
  • ✓ Even spread across bare and thin areas
  • ✓ Best results when paired with aeration
  • ✓ Watering instructions included so seed actually establishes
Starting at $60 Quoted based on coverage area and seed type

03

Fertilization

A fed lawn grows thicker, stays greener, and handles stress better.

Fertilizer isn't just about making grass grow fast — it's about giving your lawn what it needs to stay healthy through heat, drought, and heavy use. We use the right formulation for the season: higher nitrogen in the spring push, a balanced blend through summer, and a slow-release formula in the fall to carry roots through winter.

We don't use the same bag on every yard. What goes down depends on what your lawn actually needs.

  • ✓ Season-appropriate formulation every application
  • ✓ Even spread with no streaking or burning
  • ✓ Can be scheduled as a one-time or seasonal program
  • ✓ Safe for kids and pets once dry
Starting at $45 Per application, based on lawn size
❌
04

Weed Control

Dandelions, crabgrass, clover, creeping charlie — we target what's actually growing in your yard.

Broadcast spraying everything isn't the right move. Different weeds need different treatments, and some require timing to work properly. Pre-emergent goes down in early spring before weeds germinate. Post-emergent takes care of what's already up. We use the right approach based on what we're dealing with.

The goal is to kill the weeds without stressing the grass — and to keep them from coming back, not just knock them down for a few weeks.

  • ✓ Targeted application — we treat the weeds, not just the lawn
  • ✓ Pre-emergent and post-emergent options depending on timing
  • ✓ Common Genesee County weeds: dandelion, crabgrass, clover, creeping charlie
  • ✓ Safe for lawns, kids, and pets once dry
Starting at $50 Per application, based on lawn size and weed pressure

⚡ Better Together

Some Treatments Work Best as a Pair

Aeration + overseeding is the most effective thing you can do for a thin or struggling lawn — the seed falls into the aeration holes and gets direct soil contact, which is exactly what it needs to germinate. Fertilization after seeding gives new grass the nutrients to establish quickly. We'll tell you what combinations make sense for your situation.

Aeration + Overseeding

Best for thin or patchy lawns

Weed Control + Fertilization

Kill weeds, feed the grass that's left

Aeration + Fertilization

Open up the soil, then feed it deep
