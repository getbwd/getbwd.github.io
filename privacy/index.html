Legal Compliance Statement

We voluntarily adopt additional protections consistent with emerging privacy standards to ensure transparency and consumer trust.

Information We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal information: (1) personal identifiers (full name, phone number, email address, and property/service address), (2) service information (requested services, property details relevant to service performance, and scheduling and service history), (3) payment information and billing details (processed securely via third-party processors; we do not store full payment card data), (4) technical and usage data (IP address, browser type, device information, and website interaction data), (5) communications (messages, inquiries, or feedback you send to us and call or text records for customer service and quality assurance), (6) sensitive information (we do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data (such as Social Security numbers or financial account numbers), except where absolutely necessary and explicitly provided).

How We Collect Information

We collect information through direct user input (forms, calls, texts, bookings), automatic technologies (cookies, analytics tools), and third-party platforms (e.g., social media, advertising services).

We use your information for legitimate business purposes, including providing and managing services, scheduling and customer communication, processing payments, improving service quality and operations, marketing and promotional communications (where permitted), and legal compliance and fraud prevention.

We only collect data that is relevant and reasonably necessary for these purposes, consistent with standard privacy principles.

Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We may share your information only in the following circumstances: (1) service providers (with trusted third-party vendors who assist in payment processing, scheduling or CRM systems, and marketing and analytics. All third parties are contractually required to safeguard your data), (2) legal compliance (we may disclose information to comply with laws, subpoenas, legal processes, or to protect rights, safety, and property), (3) business transfer (in the event of a merger, sale, or restructuring, your data may be transferred as part of business assets).

Data Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect your information. This includes secure systems and restricted access, encryption where appropriate, and monitoring for unauthorized access.

Michigan law requires businesses to protect personal data and prevent misuse of identifiable information. However, no system is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy, including providing services, maintaining business and financial records, complying with legal obligations, resolving disputes, and enforcing agreements.

Unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law, we apply the following general retention standards: (1) customer contact and service records are retained for a minimum of three (3) years after the last completed service, (2) financial and transaction records are retained for a minimum of seven (7) years to comply with tax and accounting obligations, (3) and communication records (including emails, messages, and call logs) may be retained for up to three (3) years for customer service, dispute resolution, and quality assurance purposes.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, each applicable retention period shall recommence upon any subsequent customer interaction with BWD Services, including but not limited to account access, service requests, or completed services.

At the end of the applicable retention period, or when the information is no longer required for a legitimate business or legal purpose, we will securely dispose of such information using commercially reasonable and industry-standard methods designed to prevent unauthorized access, use, or disclosure. Such methods may include permanent deletion of electronic records from active systems and backups where feasible, secure overwriting or wiping of digital storage media, and physical destruction or shredding of paper records.

We take reasonable steps to ensure that personal information cannot be reconstructed, retrieved, or otherwise accessed after disposal.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, we may retain certain information for a longer period if required to comply with legal obligations, enforce our agreements, detect or prevent fraud, or protect the rights, property, or safety of BWD Services, our customers, or others.
